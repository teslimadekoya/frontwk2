Lenna Gunning-Williams

Professional Footballer

Lenna Gunning-Williams isn’t just a baller — she’s the kind of player who makes defenders wish they’d stayed home. Born in London in 2005, Lenna’s been lighting it up since her academy days at Tottenham Hotspur, racking up goals like it’s second nature. She made her senior debut at just 17, and has been on a steady rise ever since — including a standout loan at Ipswich Town where she proved she’s just getting started.
But Lenna’s not all boots and goals. Ever seen Jamie Johnson? Yep, that was her playing Jack Marshall — the fearless girl who took on the boys and chased her football dreams. Art imitating life, much? Whether she’s scoring screamers or showing off her personality on socials, Lenna brings joy, confidence, and pure vibes wherever she goes. She’s not just one to watch — she’s the one changing the game.

Some facts about her:

  • She started young – Lenna joined the Tottenham Hotspur academy and scored 38 goals in just 39 games for their under-21s. Talk about making an entrance!
  • She’s a baller and an actress – Before breaking into pro football, she starred as Jack Marshall in the CBBC series Jamie Johnson, a role that mirrored her real-life football journey.
  • Pro at 18 – In July 2023, Lenna signed her first professional contract with Tottenham Hotspur. Big moves for a big talent!
  • Loan star – She spent the 2023–24 season on loan at Ipswich Town and bagged 14 goals in all competitions — proving she’s got the magic touch anywhere she goes.
  • Confidence queen – Lenna’s known for her fearless style on the pitch, never afraid to take on defenders or try something bold.
  • Admin for a day? Sure! – She once took over Spurs’ social media for a day, showing off her playful side and letting fans in on the fun.
  • Multi-talented – Whether it's scoring goals or being on screen, Lenna brings energy, focus, and charisma to everything she does.
  • Inspo in boots – She’s become a role model for young girls in football, proving that you can chase your dreams — and smash them, too.
